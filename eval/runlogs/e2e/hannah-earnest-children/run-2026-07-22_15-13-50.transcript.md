# Locality Guide: West Bromwich (in Staffordshire), England (1870–1901)

## Jurisdiction overview
- **Town:** West Bromwich, a large industrial town in south Staffordshire (the "Black Country")
- **Civil Registration District:** West Bromwich, formed 1 July 1837 when national civil registration began
- **County:** Staffordshire (pre-1974); transferred to the new Metropolitan County of West Midlands in 1974 — genealogy records are organized under historic Staffordshire
- **Economy:** Iron and steel manufacturing, coal mining, anchor and chain making; typical Black Country industrial occupations
- **Religious denominations:** Church of England (Anglican), Nonconformist chapels (Methodist, Baptist, Congregationalist)

## Boundary notes
- West Bromwich remained in Staffordshire for the entire period 1870–1901; the 1974 boundary change to West Midlands does **not** affect record organization for this research
- FamilySearch wiki and genealogy databases organize West Bromwich records under historic **Staffordshire** — search accordingly

## Available record types

### Civil registration (GRO, from 1 July 1837)
- **What:** Birth, marriage, and death certificates issued by local registrar (West Bromwich district)
- **Index access (free):** FreeBMD — https://www.freebmd.org.uk — covers most years for 1870–1901 births; searchable by surname, first name, district, and quarter
- **Index access (subscription):** Ancestry "England & Wales Birth Index, 1837–2005"; FindMyPast; MyHeritage "England & Wales Birth Index, 1837–2005" — all **indexed + images of index pages**
- **Certificate ordering:** GRO online ordering service (https://www.gro.gov.uk) — full certificates include mother's maiden name, father's occupation, informant
- **Note:** The birth index does NOT include mother's maiden name until 1912. For 1877–1891 Grice births, search by child surname "Grice" in West Bromwich / Staffordshire registration district
