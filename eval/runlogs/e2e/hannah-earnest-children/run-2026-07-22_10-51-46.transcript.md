# Locality Guide: West Bromwich (Staffordshire) & Birmingham (Warwickshire), England (c. 1870–1915)

## Jurisdiction overview
- **West Bromwich**: Municipal borough in Staffordshire until 1974, when it was incorporated into the new metropolitan county of West Midlands. Within the **West Bromwich civil registration district** for GRO births/deaths; part of the Black Country industrial region.
- **Birmingham**: City in Warwickshire until 1974 (West Midlands thereafter). Its own civil registration district from 1837. A major industrial and manufacturing centre.
- The Grice family was in **West Bromwich** for children born 1877–1886, and in **Birmingham** by 1891. GRO birth registrations for pre-1891 children will be in the West Bromwich district; for post-1891 children in the Birmingham district.
- **Key historical note**: England and Wales civil registration began **1 July 1837**. All births, marriages, and deaths in this period must have been registered by law.

## Boundary changes
- West Bromwich was in Staffordshire for the entire period under research (1870–1915); no boundary changes affect record location in this window.
- Birmingham was in Warwickshire through 1915; no relevant boundary changes.
- Post-1974 reorganisation is irrelevant to finding pre-1915 records.

## Available record types

### Vital records — Civil registration (PRIMARY source for additional children)
- **Start date**: 1 July 1837 nationwide
- **What exists**: Birth, marriage, and death registrations. Births include child's name, date/place, father's name/occupation, mother's name (including maiden name). **Mother's maiden name "Earnest" is searchable in indexes from 1911 onwards; earlier indexes have only child's surname and district.**
- **GRO Birth Registration Index 1837–2008** — at FamilySearch (collection 2285338), free, indexed; also at Ancestry (8912, 8782), MyHeritage (10442), FreeBMD (free online). Searchable by surname "Grice" + district "West Bromwich" or "Birmingham" + approximate year.
- **FreeBMD** (freebmd.org.uk) — free index of GRO registers 1837–1915; most complete for this period.
- **GRO website** (gro.gov.uk) — certified copies of original certificates can be ordered (for full detail including mother's maiden name in all periods).
- **Gap note**: The index alone does not give mother's maiden name before 1911 for births; a certificate must be ordered to confirm parentage. Search by surname "Grice" in the West Bromwich and Birmingham districts and verify with certificates.

### Church records (baptisms/burials — Secondary; supplement civil registration)
