# Mary Reese — siblings

**Source PID:** `G9VJ-1WS`
**Mary Reese is deceased.** (FamilySearch ToS requires all committed
e2e fixtures to be about deceased persons.)

## Research question

> Who were the siblings of Mary Reese, born 1880 in New York and died
> 22 June 1933?

## What was removed from the starting tree

Because siblings sit two hops from the subject, the unstripped tree was
snapshotted from a **parent** (Lewis Joseph Reese) to capture the full
sibling set, then the siblings were stripped:

- Removed all **nine sibling persons** and their parent-child links to
  Lewis Joseph Reese and Laura Amelia Scott: Clarence (1869), Elizabeth
  Jane (1875), Lewis (1878), Lora A. (1883), Martin Joseph (1885), Jenny
  (1887), Fanny (1888), Richard (1890), and Emily Florence (1892) Reese.
- Removed the **records that name a sibling** — the siblings' own
  marriage, birth, and death records (10 sources). No sibling given name
  remains anywhere in the starting tree.

## What was left intact as anchors

- Mary Reese and her **parents** Lewis Joseph Reese and Laura Amelia
  Scott, plus her **grandparents** Lewis Moses Reese and Elizabeth Jane
  Ritchie.
- The **census households** of Lewis/Louis Reese (1880, 1892 NY state,
  1910) — their citations name only the parents, but reading the records
  re-reveals the children (siblings) living at home. These are the
  rediscovery path.
- Mary's own 1898 marriage and 1933 death records.

## Expected difficulty

medium — the siblings are recoverable by working out from the parents:
the 1880 census household names the early siblings (Clarence, Lizzie,
Lewis), and the 1892/1900 censuses cover those born later.

## Findings structure

Three siblings the 1880 census gives directly are **required**
(f1 Clarence, f2 Elizabeth Jane, f3 Lewis); the six born after 1880 are
**bonus** (f4–f9: Lora, Martin, Jenny, Fanny, Richard, Emily) — they
require the 1892/1900 censuses.

## Notes for reviewers

Reachability was confirmed before authoring: a census search for the
Louis/Lewis Reese household (father Lewis, mother Laura, NY, 1880)
returns the household naming Clarence, Lizzie, Louis, and May (Mary) as
the top hit. The subject snapshot returns only the parents (siblings are
two hops out), so the unstripped tree was taken from the parent
(source_pid remains the subject, Mary).
