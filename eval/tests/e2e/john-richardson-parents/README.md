## Research question

> Who were the parents of John Richardson, born 24 July 1833 in Preston,
> Lancashire, England, an LDS pioneer who emigrated via Nauvoo to Utah
> and died 10 March 1906 in Richmond, Cache County, Utah?

## Expected answer

- **Father:** Thomas Richison (also recorded Richardson / Richeson /
  Richardsen) — b. 18 February 1804, Wigan, Lancashire, England; d. 3
  November 1886, Richmond, Cache County, Utah.
- **Mother:** Mary Mallalieu (married name Richardson) — b. 16 September
  1806, Chorley, Lancashire, England; d. 9 March 1846, Nauvoo, Hancock
  County, Illinois.

The **mother is the hard crux**: she died in Nauvoo in 1846, *before*
the family reached Utah, so she appears in **none** of the abundant Utah
records (censuses, militia files, death certificates, obituaries). She
must be recovered from early Nauvoo community records and English
(Chorley) parish registers.

**What was deliberately kept as legitimate search anchors.** John's own
birth and christening facts (Preston / Ashton-under-Lyne, 1833) and his
Nauvoo (1839–1846) and Pottawattamie, Iowa (1850) residence facts remain
as leads; so do his spouse (Lola Ann Amelia Tyler), all eleven children,
the Utah censuses of his own household (1860/1870/1880/1900), militia
files, children's death certificates and obituaries (which name John as
a *parent*, never his own parents), and his burial evidence. **The 1850
U.S. census (household of Thomas Richison, Pottawattamie, Iowa) is
kept** — it lists John (age 18, b. England) as a co-resident of Thomas,
a genuine lead to the **father**, but as census co-residence it *states*
no relationship, and the adult woman in the 1850 household is **Anna**
(Thomas's later wife / stepmother), **not** the birth-mother Mary — so
the census does not leak the mother and even sets a productive
stepmother trap.

## How the answer is recoverable (records only; person-tree reads blocked)

- **Father.** John's retained 1850 residence in Pottawattamie, Iowa
  leads to the 1850 census household of **Thomas Richison** (b. England,
  ~1804), placing an England-born John with an England-born Thomas —
  corroborated by Thomas's own 1886 death and burial at Richmond, Cache
  County (the same town where John died) and by their shared 1852
  emigration. The removed Nauvoo Community Project entry is
  independently re-findable by a fresh search of Nauvoo records.
- **Mother (hard).** John's retained Nauvoo residence (1839–1846) is the
  lead. Searching Nauvoo-era records surfaces the family reconstruction
  naming **Mary Mallalieu** (variously *Millenew* / *Mallalew*), who
  died at Nauvoo in 1846; English parish records for Chorley, Lancashire
  corroborate her 1806 origin. She is absent from every Utah record
  because she never emigrated, which is what makes her the hardest link.

All live on FamilySearch; none requires reading the (blocked) person
tree.

## Expected difficulty

hard — The father is reachable through the retained 1850 census and his
own Utah death, but under heavy surname-spelling variation
(Richison / Richeson / Richardsen / Richardson / Richards). The mother
is genuinely hard: her pre-migration 1846 death removes her from all the

records and English parish registers rather than the obvious Utah record

## Notes for reviewers

Ground truth (from the FamilySearch tree): father Thomas "Thos" Richison
(b. 18 Feb 1804 Wigan, Lancashire, England; d. 3 Nov 1886 Richmond,
Cache, Utah), mother Mary Mallalieu (married name Richardson; b. 16 Sep
1806 Chorley, Lancashire, England; d. 9 Mar 1846 Nauvoo, Hancock,
Illinois). The MOTHER is the hard crux: she died in Nauvoo in 1846,
before the family reached Utah, so she appears in NONE of the abundant
Utah records (census, militia, death certificate, obituary). Recovering
her requires early Nauvoo community records and English parish registers
(Chorley chr. 1806). The father is the medium half: he IS in Utah
records but under surname spelling variants (Richison/Richeson/
Richardsen) and has a tangled marital history (a "separation from Anna
Owen" event, 1855; Anna is the woman shown with him in the 1850 census).
John's own English christening is recorded under the surname "Richards"
at Ashton-under-Lyne though he was born in Preston — a spelling+place
mismatch. Both parents and John share a documented Nauvoo residence
(1839-1846) and a joint 1852 emigration, giving a strong migration/FAN
correlation once the England-Utah bridge is made.
