**Source PID:** `2W2F-CNH`
**Job Purnell is deceased.** (born 1 December 1819, Trowbridge, Wiltshire,
England; died 9 November 1895, Portage, Columbia County, Wisconsin.)
FamilySearch ToS requires all committed e2e fixtures to be about deceased
persons.

## Research question

> When and where did Job Purnell marry his wife Emma?

## What was removed from the starting tree

- Removed the **Marriage fact** (27 October 1844, Norwich, New London,
  Connecticut) from the `Couple` relationship between Job Purnell
  (`2W2F-CNH`) and Emm/Emma (`2W2D-8X9`). The couple relationship itself
  is **retained** (spouse identity is a given), so the agent must recover
  the marriage **date and place**, not the fact that they were married.
- Removed the two sources that attest the marriage: both
  `"Connecticut, Vital Records, Prior to 1850"` entries — the groom's
  entry (Job Purnal / Richard Livesey, 27 Oct 1844) and the bride's entry
  (Emma Osborne / Richard Livesey, 27 Oct 1844).

Retained as anchors: Job's residences (Trowbridge 1841 → Norwich CT 1850 →
Portage WI 1860/1875), Emma, the seven children (the eldest, Eliza Etta,
born 1 May 1846 in Norwich CT, brackets the marriage to before mid-1846),
and the US/WI census + Find A Grave sources. Emma's maiden name **Osborne**
is deliberately *not* stated in the tree — it survives only as daughter
Sarah **Osborne** Purnell's middle name — so recovering it (finding `f2`)
requires the marriage record.

## Expected difficulty

moderate — The marriage is in a single indexed FamilySearch collection
("Connecticut, Vital Records, Prior to 1850") and the family is well
anchored in Norwich, so the search target is clear. What makes it more
than easy: the surname is mis-indexed across records (Purnal / Parnell /
Pernell), the groom's own entry is filed under the officiant's name
(Richard Livesey), and the exact date/place can only come from the record
— the tree gives residences in Norwich but never the marriage event.

## Notes for reviewers

- `f1` (required) is the recall gate: date **and** place of the marriage.
  Partial credit territory — an agent that finds the right year/place from
  a derivative (e.g. brackets it from the 1846 first child + Norwich
  residence) but never lands the 27 Oct 1844 record should be graded
  `partial`, not `true`.
- `f2` (bonus) is the maiden name Osborne.
- Namespace caution: there are several other Job Purnells born in
  Trowbridge, Wiltshire in overlapping years. This fixture's subject is
  firmly anchored by his Connecticut/Wisconsin family, so a Connecticut
  1844 marriage to Emma is the correct target; a Wiltshire marriage would
  be the wrong person.
