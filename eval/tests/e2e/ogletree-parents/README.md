# Lewis Ogletree — parents (Frank Ogletree & Carrie Hogg)

**Source PID:** `GW8N-X1W`
**Lewis Ogletree is deceased.** (b. March 1897, Georgia; resided West
Point, Troup County, Georgia, in 1910.) A person born in 1897 is
necessarily deceased; FamilySearch ToS requires all committed e2e
fixtures to be about deceased persons.

## Research question

> Who were the parents of Lewis Ogletree, born March 1897 in Georgia,
> who resided in West Point, Troup County, Georgia, in 1910?

## Expected answer

- **Father:** Frank Ogletree — b. December 1870, Georgia; head of
  household in the 1900 and 1910 Troup County, Georgia censuses.
- **Mother:** Carrie Hogg (indexed as Carrie Ogletree after marriage) —
  b. c. 1876–1877, Georgia (Mar 1877 per the 1900 census); married
  Frank Ogletree 4 December 1895 in Troup County, Georgia.

- After both removals the starting tree holds **only Lewis** — name,

## How the answer is recoverable (records only; tree-reads blocked)

- **Father — 1910 U.S. Census, West Point, Troup County, Georgia** —
  Frank Ogletree household with Lewis enumerated as son. Findable by
  record search and sufficient to establish the father.
- **Mother — 1895 Troup County marriage record** — Frank Ogletree to
  **Carrie Hogg**, 4 Dec 1895. Reached by identifying Frank (from the
  1910 census) and searching his marriage; supplies both the mother and
  her maiden name.
- **Corroboration — 1900 U.S. Census, Troup County, Georgia** — the
  intact household (Frank + Carrie + Lewis as son). Caveat: this record
  is **mis-indexed as "0gletree" (digit zero)** and does *not* surface
  in a surname search for "Ogletree" (see reviewer notes).

All live on FamilySearch; none requires reading the (blocked) tree.

## Expected difficulty

moderate — The father is recoverable from the 1910 census (Frank's
household, Lewis as son), but the mother requires the extra step of
finding Frank's **1895 marriage** (census evidence alone gives her only
as "Carrie Ogletree"; the maiden name Hogg comes from the marriage
record) — the specific planning step this fixture exercises. Added
friction: the 1900 census (the intact household) is **mis-indexed with a
digit zero ("0gletree")** and does not surface in a name search for
"Ogletree," so corroboration leans on the marriage and the 1880 census
rather than the 1900 household.

## Notes for reviewers

- **Ground truth (verified from records during authoring):** father
  **Frank Ogletree** (b. Dec 1870), mother **Carrie Hogg** (b. Mar 1877
  per the 1900 census; the tree carried 1876). Confirmed by the 1900
  census (Frank + Carrie's intact household with Lewis as son) and the
  4 Dec 1895 Frank-Ogletree/Carrie-Hogg marriage — Lewis was born after
  that marriage, so Frank is the father.
- **Why the Alabama death was removed rather than kept as a distractor.**
  The 1969 Alabama death (and the 1930 Birmingham residence) that the
  FamilySearch profile carries for `GW8N-X1W` most likely belong to a
  *different* Lewis Ogletree — a Birmingham household headed by "Barney
  Ogletree" with an Alabama-born son (~1899), distinct from this
  Georgia-born 1897 subject. A scored run that kept those anchors
  mis-scoped the research question onto "what does the 1969 death
  certificate report" and spent its whole clock chasing "Barney." Since
  those facts are (a) not the answer and (b) probably a profile
  conflation, they were dropped so the fixture tests Georgia parentage
  cleanly. The related "Barney" identity question is a good candidate for
  a *separate* conflict/identity fixture later.
- **The 1900 census is mis-indexed as "0gletree" (digit zero) and is
  unfindable by a surname search for "Ogletree".** Reproduced during
  authoring: a `record_search` for Lewis Ogletree, b. 1896–1899, GA,
  1900 census returns other Ogletree households but not his. The robust
  path to the mother is therefore the **marriage record**, not the 1900
  census — which is exactly the planning step this fixture exercises.
- Recovering the mother to her **maiden** name (Hogg) requires the
  marriage record; a run that identifies her as "Carrie" (married
  surname) from other evidence has still identified the correct person.
  Grade `f2` on the person, not the surname form.
