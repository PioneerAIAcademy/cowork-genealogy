# Mary L. Ruse — children

**Source PID:** `L87N-ZCW`
**Mary L. Ruse is deceased.** (FamilySearch ToS requires all committed

## Research question

> What were the names of the children of Mary L. Ruse, wife of Albert
> Raney, of Loudon Township, Seneca County, Ohio?

**Identity note.** The task was submitted as "Mary L. Ruse, b. 16 Nov 1882,
d. 1915." The person at PID L87N-ZCW is Mary L. Ruse but her tree vitals
differ: born ~1855, married Albert Raney in 1878, and she appears in the
1920/1930/1940 censuses and an SSN record (so the "died 1915" fact looks
erroneous — the record may be partly conflated). The fixture is built
around the person at the PID; her exact vitals don't affect a
"who were the children" question.

## What was removed from the starting tree

- Removed all **child persons** and their parent-child links. The tree
  held eight child entries that resolve to **five** distinct children
  (with duplicates): Ora S. Raney (1879), Armeda/Armedia Elvina Raney
  (1882), and **two daughters both born 11 April 1887** — **Mary L.
  Raney** and **Bertha Belle Raney** (most likely twins; see the
  identity note below) — and Robert M. Raney (1891).
- Removed the **records that name a child** — the Ohio birth/christening
  entries (both the "Births and Christenings" and "County Births"
  versions), the children's own marriage records (Ora, Bertha), a death
  record naming a child as informant, and a 1940 census of a married
  daughter's household. Twenty source entries in all.

No child given name (Ora, Armeda, Bertha, Robert) remains anywhere in the
starting tree.

## What was left intact as anchors

- Mary L. Ruse and her husband **Albert Raney**, with their marriage.
- The **Albert Raney household censuses** (1880, 1910, 1920, 1930) — their
  citations name only Albert and Mary, but reading the census records
  re-reveals the children living at home (the intended rediscovery path).
- The Mary–Albert Ohio marriage records (1878).

## Expected difficulty

medium — the children are recoverable, but the researcher must reconstruct
the family and dedupe. The strongest path: Ohio birth records name the
mother "Mary L. Ruse," so a search by mother returns the children
(Ora 1879, Mary L. 1887, Robert 1891 are top hits); the census households
corroborate.

## Identity note — Mary L. Raney and Bertha Belle Raney are twins, not one person

An earlier draft of this fixture **merged** the two 1887 daughters into a
single finding, on the theory that "Mary L. Raney" (on a birth record) and
"Bertha Belle Raney" (on marriage records) were one person recorded under
two names. **That was wrong**, and this version corrects it. The two are
most likely **twins** — both born 11 April 1887 in Loudon Township, both
children of Albert Raney and Mary L. Ruse:

- **Mary L. Raney** appears only as a sparse 1887 birth record (mother
  "Mary L. Ruse"); no later life events.
- **Bertha Belle Raney** lived a full, separately documented life: married
  David Fruth (1906), appears in the 1910/1920/1930/1940 censuses,
  remarried Albert E. Whitney (1932), and **died 12 March 1973** in
  Fostoria, Hancock County, Ohio (Find A Grave). A person who lived to
  1973 cannot be the same as a daughter who is otherwise invisible after
  her 1887 birth.

**You cannot decide same-vs-twin from the mother's name alone** — the
mother is indexed variously as "Mary L. Ruse", "Lavina Riese", and
"Louisa Ruth", any of which could in principle be a different woman. The
distinguishing check is the **father**: Bertha's own marriage records name
**father Albert Raney** (1906 Fruth marriage and 1932 Whitney marriage,
*Ohio, County Marriages, 1789-2016*), tying her to the same couple as Mary
L. — so both are daughters of Albert Raney + Mary L. Ruse, and the two
1887 birth dates make them twins rather than duplicates of one child. This
both-parent extraction is exactly the work finding **f4** requires.

## Munged-tree caveat

The FamilySearch tree for this family has been merged many times and is
**partly conflated**: Mary L. Ruse's own vitals disagree across records
(a spurious "died 1915" alongside 1920/1930/1940 census appearances), the
mother's name varies by record, and the child list contains duplicates
(three separate "Bertha" entries, one dying 1918). **The tree is a lead,
not proof.** Every child relationship in the findings below is grounded in
the underlying **records** (birth records naming the mother; marriage
records naming both parents), not in the merged tree's own child links —
and the agent is expected to do the same rather than copy the tree.
