## Expected difficulty

hard — The kept anchors all point to Ohio (every residence 1820–1840, the
deed, the probate, and an Ohio burial / Find a Grave), so the surface
reading is "she never left." To establish the migration the agent must
correlate records it has to find fresh: the sparse BLM Land Tract Book
entry for "Christiana Clark" in Wisconsin (1847), her son Joseph's
Wisconsin marriage (1854), and her sons Joseph and William in the
1849–1850 Green County, Wisconsin census. Husband William Clark
predeceased her (estate probated in Coshocton, OH), so she migrated as a
widow.

## Notes for reviewers

The migration is **real**, not a tree error. Per the Find a Grave
memorial (ID 154768466), Christiana died in **Monroe, Green County,
Wisconsin** in 1848 and her remains were returned to the family plot at
**West Carlisle Cemetery, Coshocton County, Ohio** — a "died away, buried
home" pattern. The Ohio burial is therefore a legitimate confounder, not
a contradiction.

Grading:
- **f1 (required)** — she migrated to / was present in Wisconsin. This is
  recoverable from FamilySearch records (BLM land tract book 1847; son
  Joseph's WI marriage; sons in the WI census). A run that concludes she
  stayed in Ohio, or that the evidence is merely inconclusive, misses.
- **f2 (bonus)** — the exact death locality (Monroe, Green County). That
  specific place lives on the off-FamilySearch Find a Grave memorial page
  and the tree; the FamilySearch-indexed Find a Grave record shows only
  the Ohio burial, so exact-place credit is bonus, not required.
