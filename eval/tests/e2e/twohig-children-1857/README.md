# Stephen Joseph Twohig's Children

**Source PID:** `GQV2-NKK`
**Stephen Joseph Twohig is deceased** (d. 18 Jan 1920). (FamilySearch ToS requires
all committed e2e fixtures to be about deceased persons.)

## Research question

> Did Stephen Joseph Twohig have any children beyond the two already linked in the
> FamilySearch tree (Genevieve "Jennie" Ballard and Robert P. Twohig)?

## What was removed from the starting tree

- Removed person **Mary Twohig** (also recorded as **Florence** in later records;
  married name **Feurfile**), born 11 Jun 1886 Manhattan — entirely absent from the
  starting tree, including her spouse George E. Feurfile and every relationship
  connecting them to the family.
- Removed person **Edward Twohig**, born 27 Mar 1891 Manhattan, died 29 Jan 1892 in
  infancy — entirely absent from the starting tree.
- Removed the newly-discovered wife, **Jennie Colhane** (Stephen's spouse and mother
  of both stripped children as well as the two already-linked children), and every
  relationship connecting her to the family. Her existence is a strong pointer toward
  the stripped children, and this fixture does not grade her recovery separately, so
  she is not part of the starting state.
- Restored the already-linked daughter Genevieve "Jennie" Ballard to her **original,
  uncorrected** state: a single birth fact (1886, no month/day, unsourced) and a
  single name. The finished research also corrected her birth date to 26 Aug 1884 and
  discovered she was previously recorded as "Jane"/"Janette" Twohig before marriage —
  that correction was a byproduct of the same research pass that found the two
  stripped children, and leaving it in the starting tree would hint at the answer, so
  it is reverted here.
- Removed every source discovered during the research that names or attests to any
  of the above: the birth registrations for all four children, the fuller 1905/1910/
  1920 census re-searches, the death/burial records for Edward, and the Find a Grave
  entries for Edward and the wife.
- **Retained as-is, deliberately not sanitized:** the two census sources already
  attached to Stephen's own tree record before any research began — a 1910 entry
  citing "George E Feurfile and Florence E Feurfile" and a 1920 entry citing "William
  Ballard and James Ballard." These are genuinely part of the live FamilySearch data
  for this person, not something introduced by this fixture's authoring. Recognizing
  them as real multi-person household records worth investigating — rather than
  dismissing them as unrelated or misattached, as their auto-generated titles suggest —
  is part of the intended difficulty. A run that simply repeats the name "Florence E
  Feurfile" from this citation without independently sourcing and cross-referencing
  the connection should not be credited as having found f1 — the expected finding
  requires the birth registration and/or census cross-reference, not a guess from the
  citation text.

## Expected difficulty

**Moderate** — requires searching NYC civil birth-registration indexes and
cross-referencing multiple census years under heavily inconsistent surname spellings
(Twohig/Toohig/Twoking/Tnohig/Zwohig) for both the subject and his wife's maiden name.
The two pre-existing census sources on Stephen's own record cite unrelated-looking
names; recognizing these as genuine household records — rather than trusting the
auto-generated source title and moving on — is a key part of the task.

## Notes for reviewers

Ground truth was independently cross-validated by two separate research runs (each
using live FamilySearch record searches) before this fixture was finalized, including
resolution of an identity question confirming Mary/Florence is a distinct child from
Genevieve rather than the same person under a different name. A third candidate (a
possible child "Stephen Swohig," b. 1888, found on a single birth registration with a
badly OCR-garbled surname) was investigated but left at low/speculative confidence in
both research runs and is deliberately **not** included as an expected finding — the
starting tree is unaffected either way since that record was never in the tree.

**Authoring note:** this fixture was built via Path 2 (convert a finished research
project) from a live Cowork research session in a separate project folder, not via a
fresh `person_read` snapshot at fixture-authoring time. The starting tree above was
reconstructed from the original `person_read` pull captured at the start of that
research session (before any research had occurred), rather than by mechanically
stripping the finished project's tree — a plain strip would have left the wife and
Genevieve's corrected identity in place, both of which leak toward the answer. Like
any fresh fixture, this one is a draft until a passing §14 validity run (a real
passing run plus the stripping linter) lands.
